Our herbal studies are a constant reminder of the way Mother Nature takes care of us. Take the unassuming Calendula also known as garden marigold or pot marigold. Just looking at the sunshine splayed orange and yellow petals warms my heart and makes me smile. Calendula has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, astringent, antifungal, antiviral, and immunostimulant properties making it useful for disinfecting and treating minor wounds, conjunctivitis, cuts, scrapes, chapped or chafed skin, bruises, burns, athleteâ€™s foot, acne, yeast infections, bee stings, diaper rashes, and other minor irritations and infections of the skin. Calendula stimulates the production of collagen at wound sites to help minimize scarring and stretch marks. This versatile botanical can be incorporated into baths, creams, compresses, washes, salves, ointments, message oils, facial steams, tinctures, and teas. A powerful medicine gentle enough to use for babies, children or animals.
